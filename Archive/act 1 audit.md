### What's Working

1. **Pace is excellent.** Original Act 1 is ~25,000 words (34% of the book). The condensed Act 1 is roughly 6,500-7,000 words. That's a 70%+ reduction and it still hits all the plot beats.
    
2. **Third person works.** More pace, no loss of emotion. Steve's grief comes through fine.
    
3. **The humour is sharper.** "Nibbled to death by ducklings," the rabbit/cow/top-hat riff, "Lisa Plant," the Tooth Fairy/Easter Bunny — these are punchier than the original.
    
4. **"That Man Is an Arse" is the standout.** The expanded Vrinda conversation is arguably better than the original. The scope expansion discussion ("what would have happened to scope if the date had been December next year?") is a key concept delivered through dialogue rather than exposition.
    
5. **The structural change with Craig is smart.** Deferring all Craig's teaching to Part Two (when Steve is desperate) is cleaner narratively. In the original, Steve gets a full education (EC, Dog Food, French Fry) BEFORE the mutiny, then walks out and comes crawling back. The condensed version has him briefly meet Craig, not pay attention, walk out — then the crisis escalates — then he's desperate enough to actually listen. Tighter arc.
    
6. **The mutiny merger works perfectly.** Two chapters into one, no loss.

### What's Lost (and Whether It Matters)

**Losses that hurt:**

- **The Scooby-Doo moment.** Alison's "They're all like that, Dad" is one of the most memorable scenes in the book. It's thematic foreshadowing (the "monster" — the speed/quality trade-off — isn't really a monster). It's a warm family moment. And it's the kind of quirky detail readers remember. **I'd put this back.** Could be 3-4 sentences inside "Ahoy, Me Mateys."
    
- **Craig's sticky note (Ch 8).** Steve crumples Craig's URGENT message and bins it. It's tiny — two paragraphs in the original — but it's a perfect beat showing Steve's stubbornness. And it pays off later when Steve has to grovel. **Worth restoring.** One paragraph at the end of "The War Room."
    

**Losses that are fine:**

- **Craig's Wyx-Health backstory (Ch 5).** In the original, Steve calls Craig and hears the buy-vs-build story. In the condensed version, Norbert nags but Steve never calls. The Wyx-Health story isn't needed — it was setup for the EC, which is now deferred anyway.
    
- **Gregor droning through the Gantt chart (Ch 6).** Vrinda challenging him about low-priority features is a nice beat, but the condensed version shows Gregor's controlling nature well enough through his compulsory overtime decisions and "That Man Is an Arse."
    
- **The full Evaporating Cloud teaching in Act 1 (Ch 13-15).** This was always a pace-killer in the original — six chapters of Craig teaching before the crisis fully arrives. Deferring it to Part Two is the right call. The memory says "DON'T teach the Evaporating Cloud" anyway (refer to "corkscrew solutions" instead).
    
- **Steve's promise to Declan on the walk back (Ch 21).** A small loss. The condensed version has Declan deliver the December 1st news but skips the "no forced overtime" promise. It's already been established through Vrinda's deal.

### Structural Observation

The condensed draft reframes Act 1 as a pure escalation machine:

**Bad news → Worse news → Even worse → Team meeting → War room → Recovery plan → But the plan won't work → Mutiny → WAR DECLARED**

It strips out the "learning" thread (Craig's teaching) that the original weaves through the middle of Act 1. This is a good instinct for "the movie version" — you want Act 1 to be all escalating tension, then Act 2 is where the mentor appears and the learning begins.
