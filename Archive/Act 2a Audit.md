## What's New in the Condensed Version

### 1. The 2x2 Grid (Speed vs Quality)
The original doesn't have this. Craig says *"Sacrificing quality almost always slows things down"* (Ch 29) but never draws a grid. The condensed version ADDS Craig sketching a napkin grid — slow/fast vs high/low quality — showing the "top-right corner" everyone thinks is impossible.

**Verdict:** Excellent addition. It replaces the Evaporating Cloud as Steve's visual thinking tool. Simpler, more memorable, and it pays off beautifully at the end when Steve uses it to negotiate his way into both jobs. Keep it.

### 2. Norbert says "Start looking for a new job"
This line IS in the original (Ch 22, line 127), but the condensed version makes it the chapter title and gives it more weight. The original buries it; the condensed version makes it land like a gut-punch.

**Verdict:** Good instinct. Raises the stakes.

### 3. Packing Peanuts
This IS from the original (Ch 29, line 103). Craig coins the term. Faithfully preserved.

## What's Cut and Fine to Leave Cut

### Chapter 27: "The Lesson"
Craig explicitly links cafeteria lessons to software. In the condensed version, this connection is woven into "Requirements Are Really Forecasts" instead of being a standalone beat. No loss.

### Chapter 30: "Things Change"
A beautiful, atmospheric chapter. Steve drives home thinking about Fran, aerial photographs of the town changing over decades, the Castle as a constant. He arrives home, puts the kids to bed (Alison's chocolate mousse joke), has dinner with Mum. Mum tells him to call Graeme (a recruiter) — pragmatic while Steve is loyal.

In the condensed version, all of this is cut. Worth restoring? No:
- The "things change" theme is implicit throughout the whole book
- Norbert already tells Steve to look for a new job (condensed line 610), so Mum echoing it would be redundant
- The Fran memories and aerial photos are lovely but not load-bearing
- The kids' bedtime scene is charming but not plot-critical

### Chapter 31: EC Detail
The original has Steve systematically working through Evaporating Cloud pros and cons at the kitchen sink. The condensed version correctly strips all EC mechanics (per the decision to NOT teach the EC) and keeps just the thinking about prioritization and hoarding. Good.

### Chapter 32: Non-functional Requirements Digression
The original has a lengthy passage about "qualities of service" vs "features." Cut in the condensed version. Good — it's teaching content that slows the pace.

### Craig's Homework (Draw the Overproduction Cloud)
Original Ch 29 has Craig assigning specific EC homework. Condensed version replaces this with Craig assigning simpler homework: "Think about how Cheryl's lessons might apply to software. Think about small batches. And call Catherine." Correctly adapted for a version that doesn't teach the EC.

### "My first batch ends on December 1st!"
In the original (Ch 29), Steve has this specific realization at the end of his meeting with Craig. In the condensed version, the small batches insight builds differently — through the kitchen sink and Ashley's cereal — arriving at the same place without this specific eureka. No loss; the condensed route is more organic.

### Norbert's threat about walking out
Original Ch 24: Craig tells Steve that Norbert said if Steve walks out again, Norbert will fly there and take over his job. Nice moment but not essential. Cut.

## Issues Found

### 🔴 ISSUE 1: The Bottleneck is in the Wrong Place (Continuity Error)

The condensed "Bottleneck" chapter (lines 949–1025) sits at the END of Part Two, BEFORE the Inverted Pyramid is introduced and BEFORE any chunks are delivered.

But it contains lines like:
- *"During the first chunk, developers were definitely the bottleneck—we found dozens of defects but could only fix a fraction."*
- *"Every time they shipped a new chunk, testers had to verify they hadn't broken anything in the previous chunks."*

These reference events that haven't happened yet in the condensed narrative. The Inverted Pyramid comes in Part Three. The first chunk delivery ("It Worked") comes at the end of Part Three.

**Fix:** Move "The Bottleneck" to Part Four ("Transformation Under Fire"), after the first chunk is delivered. It belongs in the rhythm section where the team is iterating and discovering that testing is becoming the constraint.

### 🟡 ISSUE 2: POV Slip (First Person)

Line 588: `I said, "What should I do?"`

The condensed version is third person throughout. This line is first person — carried over from the original, which is entirely first person. Should be something like: `"What should I do?" Steve asked.`

### 🟡 ISSUE 3: Minor Tense Inconsistency

Line 598: `He said that he can't help everyone`

Should be: `He said that he couldn't help everyone` (reported speech, past tense).

**Two minor text fixes:** POV slip and tense inconsistency.
