# Late Inspection vs. In-Process Inspection

## Plain English Explanation
Late inspection means testing at the end when it's expensive to fix—like tasting soup after hours of cooking and having to throw it out. In-process inspection means checking during the process—tasting the stock before putting it in, checking ingredients before adding them. The surprising result: all that extra testing doesn't take more time; it saves time by preventing expensive rework.

### Software Parallel
Testing at the end (after all coding done) vs. testing throughout development. Finding bugs early is cheaper than finding them late.

## Key Quote
> You moved from late inspections—testing or tasting at the end of the process when it's very expensive to fix stuff—to in-process inspections, where you build quality into your product during the process.
